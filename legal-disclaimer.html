RIDDOR Reporting
Statutory obligation — UK

The Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013 (RIDDOR) requires employers and self-employed persons to report certain workplace incidents to the Health and Safety Executive (HSE).

Reportable incidents include:

  • Fatalities — deaths of workers or non-workers resulting from a work-related accident
  • Specified injuries — fractures (other than fingers/thumbs/toes), amputations, loss of sight, crush injuries to head or torso, burns, scalping, loss of consciousness, any injury requiring resuscitation or overnight hospitalisation
  • Over-7-day incapacitation — where a worker cannot perform their normal duties for more than 7 consecutive days (not counting the day of the accident)
  • Over-3-day incapacitation — must be recorded (not reported) if worker cannot work for more than 3 days
  • Dangerous occurrences — specified near-miss events (scaffold collapse, explosion, contact with overhead lines, etc.)
  • Occupational diseases — including carpal tunnel syndrome, occupational dermatitis, hand-arm vibration syndrome, and others

Report to HSE
RIDDOR reports must be submitted to the HSE directly. BuildBoss PRO does not submit reports on your behalf.
→ hse.gov.uk/riddor

Failure to report a RIDDOR reportable incident is a criminal offence under UK law and may result in prosecution and significant fines.
